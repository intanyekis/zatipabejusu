Authorities worked hard to uncover the mystery of the Dover murder suicide case.

On Tuesday, March 28, 2023, two adults, Christine, and Sean Navish, were discovered dead inside a house on Dowaliby Court. As a result, police are looking into a possible murder suicide.

On March 29, 2023, the New Hampshire attorney general stated that Dover police had discovered the two deceased inside the single-family home where they resided. To check on the residents’ welfare, police were called to the house.

The house at the core of the ongoing investigation is located at 4 Dowaliby Court. 

According to Dover property records, Christine and Sean have owned the home since 2018. The couple is shown as married in documents submitted to the Strafford County Register of Deeds as recently as 2021.

With assistance from the family, they were able to gain access, according to senior assistant solicitor general Ben Agati. “Those inside weren’t responding to them at all.“

As per police, both adults lived at the house. Although the solicitor general’s office revealed their names, they were characterized as married and in their 50s. 

Before notifying the victims’ families, the state solicitor general’s office declined to confirm their identities immediately.

The attorney general’s spokeswoman, Michael Garrity, stated that additional material is anticipated to be made public once the autopsies have been completed.

The attorney general’s announcement states. “Although the investigation is in the early stages, there does not appear to be any threat to the general public concerning these two deaths.”

According to the announcement, authorities are looking into “a probable murder and suicide of the occupants living at that location.” Officials stated there doesn’t seem to be a threat to the general public.
