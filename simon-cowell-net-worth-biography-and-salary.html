Early Life

Simon Phillip Cowell was born on October 7, 1959, in Lambeth, London, and grew up in Elstree, Hertfordshire. His father, Eric Selig Phillip Cowell who lived from 1918 to 1999, was a real estate agent, property developer, and executive in the music business. While on the other hand, his mother, Julie Brett was a ballet dancer and socialite. Despite the fact that his mother was born in Poland and came from a predominantly Jewish family, Cowell’s father avoided talking to his kids about his heritage.

Along with his brother, Cowell attended Radlett Preparatory School and the independent Dover College but left after completing his GCE O levels. He earned a GCE in Sociology after graduating from Windsor Technical College after passing English Language and Literature.

Career

Simon Cowell started his career working odd jobs, getting roles in ridiculous horror movies. It wasn’t until his father, an executive at EMI Music Publishing, was able to secure him a position in the mail room that he began to feel more at home. He left to try out other jobs after failing to get a promotion, though, and then came back to EMI.

Meanwhile, prior to leaving EMI to start his own label, E&S Music, Simon worked his way up the corporate ladder. After that, he began working for Fanfare Records, where he remained for eight years before BMG acquired the parent company. Cowell subsequently established S-Records, his own record label.

Robson Green and Jerome Flynn, two actors from the UK television drama series Soldier Soldier, were persuaded to sign with him and record the song “Unchained Melody” in 1995 as a result of his persistence. They had previously performed it on the show. The duo’s song, now known as Robson & Jerome, quickly rose to the top of the UK charts and remained there for seven weeks. It became the top-selling single of 1995, and their self-titled album, which was released later that year, also achieved that distinction. Before breaking up, they put out another album, two singles, and sold a combined 7 million albums and 5 million singles. They reportedly earned him his first million, according to Cowell. He later signed acts like Westlife and Five.

Simon developed the kid-friendly version of Il Divo, Angelis, in response to the success of that group, outdoing rivals who debuted around Christmas 2006.
