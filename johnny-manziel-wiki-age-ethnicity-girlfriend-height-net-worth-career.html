Career

Johnny Manziel is a professional footballer from the United States. He is the quarterback for the FCF Zappers, a Fan Controlled Football (FCF) team. This footballer previously spent two seasons with the Cleveland Browns of the National Football League (NFL). In 2018, he also played for the Hamilton Tiger-Cats of the Canadian Football League (CFL) and the Montreal Alouettes of the CFL. Furthermore, in 2019, this footballer was a member of the Alliance of American Football’s (AAF) Memphis Express.

Similarly, the quarterback was recruited nationally as a dual-threat quarterback out of high school. As a result, this footballer made his Texas A&M Aggies debut as a redshirt freshman. It was in Kevin Sumlin’s Air Raid offense during A&M’s first Southeastern Conference (SEC) season in 2012.

Furthermore, this footballer set a number of NCAA Division I FBS and SEC records. This footballer became the first freshman and only the fifth player in NCAA history to pass for 3,000 yards and rush for 1,000 yards in the same season.

Manziel became the first freshman to win the Heisman Trophy, Manning Award, and Davey O’Brien National Quarterback Award at the end of the regular season. This football quarterback made the most of his redshirt freshman year. He accomplished this by leading Texas A&M to a 41–13 Cotton Bowl victory over Oklahoma.

This player was selected by the Browns with the 22nd overall pick in the 2014 NFL Draft. Despite his success, Manziel struggled with consistency. As a result, this player’s career was overshadowed by off-field squabbles. This player was released by the Browns at the end of the 2015 season.

During the 2016 offseason, there were more controversies. As a result, this player was not signed by another team before the start of the season. As a result, Manziel took a two-year hiatus from football before rejoining the Tiger-Cats of the Canadian Football League in 2018.

Later in the season, this player was traded to the Alouettes. Manziel, on the other hand, was released the following year and became ineligible to play in the CFL. It was decided after they determined that Manziel had violated his contract terms. During the inaugural season of the AAF, this player signed with the Express shortly after his release. This was cut short two weeks later when the AAF ceased operations.

TexAgs gave Manziel the nickname “Johnny Football” during his sophomore high school season in Texas. This moniker accompanied him to Texas A&M University. Furthermore, in 2013, this individual applied for and was granted a registered trademark for the nickname.
