Britney Spears Defends Her Controversial Memoir As It Ruins Multiple Marriages

 Posted on July 22, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  671 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Highlights

  • Britney Spears defends her memoir, stating that its goal was not to offend anyone and that she has moved on from her past.
  • The memoir reveals Britney's past relationship with Justin Timberlake, including allegations of pressure to have an abortion and infidelity.
  • Britney also implicates photographer Adnan Ghalib in the memoir, stating that she was unaware of his marital status during their relationship.

Britney Spears has made a series of shocking revelations in her memoir The Woman in Me, and though it’s caused a lot of controversy thus far (including hurting multiple marriages), the singer has no intention of apologizing.

In fact, Britney, 41, recently took to social media to defend the memoir as well as blast the press over its coverage. Posting to Twitter, Britney clarified that the goal of her memoir was “not to offend anyone by any means." The pop star said she has “moved on” from her rocky past and wants a “clean slate” in the public eye.

Britney Spears Says Her Memoir Has Given Her "Closure"

“I am here to establish it that way for the rest of my entire life !!!” she wrote. “Either way that is the last of it and s–t happens !!!” Britney said that while she knows “some” people may be “offended” by her confessions, she admits writing the memoir was good for her as it provided “closure on all things for a better future.”

“Hopefully I can enlighten people who feel particularly alone in most cases or hurt on misunderstood !!!” the “… Baby One More Time” Britney continued. “Again, my motive for this book was not to harp on my past experiences which is what the press is doing and it’s dumb and silly !!! I have moved on since then !!!”

The Woman in Me goes into detail about Britney’s childhood and rise to stardom as well as her life under the decade-long conservatorship that was lifted in 2021.

Britney Implicates Several Married Men In Her Memoir

One thing the memoir sheds a lot of light on is her past relationship with Justin Timberlake, 42. The singer reveals she became pregnant unexpectedly during their time together, but says Justin pressured her into having an abortion, which she chose to do. Britney further claims she and Justin both cheated on each other before he allegedly broke up with her via text.

Justin hasn’t publicly responded to Britney’s claims, though there’s been a lot of uproar amongst her fans for the way he allegedly treated her. Justin has been married to actress Jessica Biel, 41, since 2012, and the pair share two sons.

Britney implicates another married man in her memoir – photographer Adnan Ghalib. Their romance began in 2007, but the singer says in her new memoir she was unaware the photographer was married at the time.

Britney and Adnan eventually broke up in 2008, the same year her father, Jamie Spears, filed to put Britney in a conservatorship. The next year, Jamie got a restraining order issued against Adnan, so he had to stay away from Britney.

“I became a robot. But not just a robot — a sort of child-robot. I had been so infantilized that I was losing pieces of what made me feel like myself,” Britney says of the conservatorship in her memoir. “The conservatorship stripped me of my womanhood, made me into a child. I became more of an entity than a person onstage. I had always felt music in my bones and my blood; they stole that from me.”

Despite the controversy her book her stirred, Britney has already revealed she’s open to writing another memoir, though nothing is confirmed.
